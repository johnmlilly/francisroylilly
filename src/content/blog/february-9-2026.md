We’ve been settling into our new home over the past few weeks. The last several weeks were a bit hectic with the holidays, the new year, and our move, but it feels good to finally be here. It’s been bittersweet, but also brings a sense of peace when we sit and take it all in. We’re excited (and a bit nervous!) about what this next chapter holds for our family.

Francis continues to make incredible progress in his development. He recently had a cardiology appointment where they examined his heart for a slight murmur that had been detected while he was still in the NICU. Thanks be to God, it was undetectable this time! He’s been given the all clear in this area.

We’ve been a bit concerned about his overall gross motor development, specifically when it comes to rolling, crawling, and sitting up. He seems to have slowed down somewhat in these areas compared to the faster progress he was making earlier. However, just when we start expressing our worry and fear to each other, he seems to hear us because he’s now starting to pull himself up! It can be hard not to compare, especially since both of our girls were already crawling and moving around a lot at this age. We try to remind ourselves that Francis isn’t on a typical developmental timeline because of his history. We’re doing our best to love him as he is and support him as he learns at his own pace.

Just the other day, he was also given the go-ahead for true solid foods (bring on the bananas, avocado, crackers, and more)! The fact that he’ll be able to start solids before he’s a year old is truly incredible. We look back on how dire his outlook once was, when we weren’t even sure if he would be able to swallow on his own. Now, to see him ready and able to eat solid foods feels like yet another miracle.

On a more practical note, we’re working to find new private therapy options for his PT and OT needs, both to reduce travel time and to help with the cost of this therapy. With the new year and insurance deductibles resetting, we’ve had to take a hard look at how we can continue to support Francis while also balancing our finances. We’re thankful that he has qualified for Medicaid and are currently searching for a new clinic that has openings and who accept this type of insurance.

We’re also looking into Richmond Hope Therapy, a weeks-long intensive therapy center that we believe could significantly help his development and overall trajectory. We’ve heard many positive things about this program and hope to be scheduled at the perfect time for his development.

We could use prayers for:

- Finding new therapists soon who will help Francis continue to grow and develop
- Openings that work with our schedule at Richmond Hope Therapy
- Continued peace and trust as our family navigates this season of transition in our new home

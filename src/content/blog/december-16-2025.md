Hello all! We have been extra busy over the past couple of months due to the holidays but also because of some very exciting news!

We’re excited to share that after 5 years of searching, we finally purchased our very first home! The whole story is still incredibly surreal but it is clearly God-orchestrated much like earlier events this year. 

We first heard about the house from good friends of ours from college who mentioned that the owner next door was preparing to move. We had just begun working with our realtor (the amazing Tracey Hickey) a few weeks prior, so this timing was clearly providential. We decided to go look at the property and meet the owner, Howard. As we got to know him, we learned that his daughter had been diagnosed with a similar condition to Francis. From that moment on, we felt immediately connected to him and his home. It was so clear the love he had for his family and that was reflected in his home.

As one thing after another fell into place, it became clear that God had weaved many pieces together. We were ultimately blessed with the opportunity to purchase the home, and we are currently preparing to move at the beginning of the year! This will come with lots of changes since our new home will be 45 minutes from where we currently live. We appreciate all of the prayers for peace and a smooth transition as we build new and hopefully lasting roots for our family. 

Now for what you really want to know — how Francis is doing! Well, he has been amazingly (and unsurprisingly at this point) reaching a ton of milestones:

- He is rolling swiftly from tummy to back.

- He is able to sit up on his own for short periods of time.

- He is taking 80% of his full feeding volume through the bottle.

- He is eating solid purees!

Most recently, he has started to babble - starting with the sound “Ma-ma” 🥹

We also had a sweet moment over Thanksgiving: Francis had his first real solid food - mashed potatoes! Such a special treat for his first Thanksgiving dinner.

On the therapy front, Francis continues to amaze us and his therapists/doctors. His determination inspires us daily. Every milestone reminds us of how far he has come and really encourages us that he’ll be able to do more.

Thank you for your continued prayers, love, and support. God is moving in quiet and powerful ways, and we’re grateful for each of you who continue walking this journey with us.

Prayer intentions:

- For him to begin rolling over from back to tummy

- For him to be able to sit up on his own without assistance

- For our transition as we move and start with new therapists and routines

- For all of the babies in the NICU and their families during this holiday season that they will experience peace and consolation from our Lord and receive the support they need.

Hello all! We have been meaning to share an update for the past few weeks but so many small things have been happening that we want to add to the list to share.

It is hard to believe that Francis is now 6 months old as of October 1st. We celebrated both his reaching half a year of life along with his patron feast day (St. Francis) at the beginning of the month. A huge shoutout to Bella’s pre-school teaching assistant, Mrs. Tammy, for gifting him his St. Francis onesie.

Now that he has passed his swallow study, he has been given the green light to increase his bottle feeds much faster. He is now taking more than half of his full volume through the bottle! The rest continues to be pumped through his feeding tube.

Additionally, since he has improved with head control and sitting up (assisted), he can also eat purées!!! And boy, does he love to eat! So far, he has tried pears, carrots, and bananas. He’s a big fan of fruits and is already holding the spoon in his hands.

Over the past month, we have finally been getting into a rhythm of his therapy appointments. While Francis is showing some great fine motor development, he is still delayed in gross motor. This is one of the biggest contributing factors towards his cerebral palsy diagnosis. He recently had his 6 month follow-up with the rehabilitation medicine doctor and his Cerebral palsy diagnosis was confirmed again. However, he is showing amazing motivation and definitely showing progress as he is learning to roll over and sit up. He almost doubled his HINE assessment score as well which is good indication for him to continue improving and develop in his mobility and functions.

It is surreal to be about 4 months removed from his time in the NICU. The adrenaline seems to be finally dwindling and we are still very much in the beginning of processing all that we have gone through together. However, the realization that we need to continue praying boldly has not wavered. He continues to be a witness to all those around him and we are so proud to be his parents.

Prayer requests:

For gross motor development
Begin rolling over
Sitting up on his own
For his g-tube to be removed due to his feeding progress
For all the little progressions to witness to the full miracle of God’s healing

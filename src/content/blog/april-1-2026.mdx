I wrote these six bullet points after we learned that we were saying goodbye to Francis during Holy Week 2025.  I didn’t know exactly what I would do with them, whether I would share them at his funeral or in another format later down the road. These six things hit me and stood out to me the most during those first few weeks of his life. Now that he is healthy and about to turn one year old, I wanted to take a moment and reflect on each of them and how I’ve seen them through my son.

* He has shown me what true strength is. 
* He has increased my faith a hundred fold. 
* He’s so young and he has given me so much. 
* My little lion fighter. 
* He has shown me how to fight until the end 
* How to have complete faith and trust in God.


## He has shown me what true strength is

<Image
  src={img1}
  alt="John and Francis"
  width={800}
  height={600}
/>

Francis fought through so much during those first couple of weeks. From the catastrophic brain damage he suffered at birth to constant seizures, internal bleeding, and more, he endured it all. We often equate strength with what we can do for ourselves and how much we can handle. I often think of some of the superheroes in Marvel movies and how easily they defeat the villains they face. But real Christian strength is different—it is a posture of surrender, a total trust in God that He will take care of everything.

Francis wasn’t able to do much for himself at first, and he had to trust us, his doctors, and those around him to care for him. Jesus gave Francis the strength to endure all the trials he faced. In turn, he taught me how to turn to Jesus in my own weakness and desperation for strength. Especially in those early days, I didn’t understand the medical terms or conditions the doctors were sharing with us. It took a great deal of strength to carry those crosses early on and trust that God would take care of my son.

Looking back now, I can see how much I had to bear for my family during that first week after Francis was born. Even in his small and fragile state, my son showed me what true strength looks like.


## He has increased my faith a hundred fold
<Image
  src={img2}
  alt="John and Francis"
  width={800}
  height={600}
/>

We were fortunate enough to have Kara transferred pretty quickly from the hospital Francis was born at to Inova. The care we received there both for her and Francis was nothing but incredible. When it came time for her to discharge and go home, we knew that Francis wouldn’t be coming with us at that point. We weren’t even sure he would be coming home at all. It was the most painful drive of our lives leaving the hospital with an empty car seat in the back of our van. To drive away knowing that he wasn’t coming with us felt so wrong and messed up. Our hearts broke knowing our boy was left at the hospital alone. 

You read in the Bible about God working so many miracles. From Israel, in the desert to Jesus’s public ministry, miracles, such as healing and even rising for the dead are everywhere. There is a song called, “Same God” by Elevation Worship. It’s a powerful song about having faith and trust in God, even in hopeless situations. One verse in particular stood out to me.

You answered prayers back then
And You will answer now
You are the same God
You are the same God

Sometimes it felt that God has answered prayers back then, but not now. I would continually ask him why he was able to back then but not now for my son. I begged for the faith to see without human sight with eyes of faith. 

As Francis continued to heal, and on that miraculous Easter Sunday, I began to have a real faith that God could in fact, totally heal my son. I began to pray with people do faith that I only learned because of my son example. I would ask boldly for complete healing for my son. I would ask others to do the same. God never grows tired of us asking for his help.

## He’s so young and he has given me so much
He taught me how to be present in each moment. How to lean into everything. To not be distracted by all the to dos and tasks but just be there. With him and with the Lord. To have real hope, to not be afraid to ask God for big things, even when it seems hopeless.
It never ceases to amaze me how many people were drawn up into prayer by this little boy. I think we see so many prayers for various things now with how connected we all are, especially with social media. When we put out our desperate plea for prayers we had no idea the impact it would have on people. 

One of his primary doctors had a line I will never forget: “These little ones have so much to teach us.” I’ve never forgotten those simple yet powerful words. The little ones do indeed teach us so much about the world around us and about ourselves. Francis indeed did this and continues to do this for me. 

## My little lion fighter. 
<Image
  src={img3}
  alt="John and Francis"
  width={800}
  height={600}
/>

That night when everything happened so quickly, when it seemed like things were spiraling faster than I could process them, when all hope seemed lost, I was scared. It didn’t hit me until much later that I waited almost four hours to see my son after he was born. I remember continually asking the nurses “Can I go see him?”, but with the chaos of everything happening they weren’t able to bring me until he was about to be flown to Inova Fairfax. 

It was the hardest thing of my life to see my son on a medical stretcher. Seeing him for the first time in a state like that brought me to tears. I felt so bad for him to have gone through so much and to be essentially unconscious lying there in the staging area as we waited for the helicopter to arrive. I wondered if he could sense anything that was going on around him. I was in shock seeing my son like that. If I touched him, would it hurt him? What should I say? A thousand thoughts ran through my mind. I felt frozen, unable to move or utter a word. Looking back, I regret not talking to him or saying anything to him when I first met him. To even touch him, so he knew I was there. I recall wanting him to feel my touch and lightly rubbing his hand. I was unsure of how much I could touch him because of all the equipment attached to him and all of the medical staff surrounding him. But I still wanted him to feel the touch of another person even in that state.

As they prepared to wheel him out, I wanted to go with him so badly. I knew I needed to stay behind and be with Kara as she came out of surgery, but it felt like my heart was taken out of my chest when they took him away to the helicopter. I didn’t know what he would be like or even where he would be the next time I saw him. As the days and weeks progressed, it felt like a lifetime was lived as we navigated each new challenge. 

Despite being so small, he moved me and changed me in ways I never could have imagined.  We affectionately referred to him as our little lion. In the Chronicles of Narnia, Aslan ( a lion) is a representative of Christ. Someone who is there, calm, steady, but strong. Never abandons the kids in their darkest hour, yet also willingly lays down his own life for theirs. 

Francis would teach us how to continue to fight for him. His strength and reslience inspired me to never give up hope, no matter the odds. We affectionately referred to him as our little lion. Never abandons the kids in their darkest hour, yet also willingly lays down his own life for theirs.  His aunt drew a picture of a lion that we proudly put up in his room and never took it down. 

## He has shown me how to fight until the end 
<Image
  src={img6}
  alt="John and Francis"
  width={800}
  height={600}
/>
I’ve been a Commanders / Redskins fan my entire life. It’s been one of my great loves since I was a kid. It was the first sport I played at age 7 (I only lasted one season!) and I would often run plays by myself growing up in my parents backyard. It has continued into my adult life, albeit with much suffering as our team has not been good. The last time they won a Super Bowl was the year I was born — 1991.

Fast forward to the 2024 season, the Commanders had an incredible season where they advanced to the NFC Champsionship game for the first time since 1991. It ultimately ended in a defeat before they could reach the Super Bowl, but the run was something I’ll never forget. I have many cherished memories celebrating and cheering them on that season, especially with Fr. Peter Clem, my best friend from college, brother in Youth Apostles, and the one who baptized and confirmed Francis. It was very special season and I felt close to my son since the last time they were this good was the year I was born. To have them put together an incredible season the year he was to be born felt like a gift from God and something I couldn’t wait to share with him. 

One moment in particular was a game against the Chicago Bears in early October. The game was an ugly one, which saw the Commanders down 15-12 with one final play left. They were at the 50 yard line and all they could do was throw one final pass (a Hail Mary as it’s known in the NFL) to try and win the game. Jayden Daniels heaved a deep ball up, and miraculously it was tipped and caught by one of the Commanders players for a touchdown.

Plays like this don’t happen often in sports. We like to think they are but they are very rare. They are very rare, and when they do happen they are remembered forever. 

I often replayed this clip in my head. I told God in prayer that all we needed was one chance, one shot. Just one. I would show this play to Francis countless times. I told him that if miracles like this can happen in a football game, then certainly a miracle could happen for him, too. Even before we experienced our incredible Easter Miracle on April 20, my son taught me how to fight until the end. To never, ever, give up hope. No matter the odds. God always has the final say and nothing is outside his power or control.

## How to have complete faith and trust in God.
<Image
  src={img7}
  alt="John and Francis"
  width={800}
  height={600}
/>
There were many times it was very hard to trust God. Each day, each hour, each minute was an unknown. We would often ask ourselves “How could this happen, God? Where are you in this?” It seemed that God could never fix this, that he would never show up, and this would be impossible to come back from. Completely hopeless. Powerless.

Even in the midst of our doubt, God showed up. He showed up in many surprising, small ways. Ways we would often look at each other and ask if this was a dream or if it was really happening. Not efficient, but effective. We struggled to hold on to hope after hearing we had to say goodbye to Francis, but I will never forget what one of our good friends told us: “When you don’t have the strength to hope, let others hope for you.” God carried us in those moments, through his grace and through the people around us.

In many hard times, we can be tempted to turn away from God. How can a God who is all loving let bad things happen? “Lord where shall we go? You have the words of eternal life.” The only way we can get to the resurrection is through the cross. There is no other way. Jesus laid the path out before us. Francis had so much courage and strength to bear his crosses with faith and trust in God. What else could he do? He had to completely rely on Jesus. He had the faith of a little child, like Christ said in the Gospels. 

## End 
The power of holy memory. To not forget but to always remember what God has done, which often means remembering hard and traumatic things too.
